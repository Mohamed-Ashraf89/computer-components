Computer Components

1. The Two Main Parts: Hardware vs. Software

Every computer system is made of two things: things you can physically touch (Hardware) and things you cannot touch (Software). Hardware (The Body): This includes the physical parts like the screen, the keyboard, the chips inside, and the mouse. Software (The Mind): These are the programs and instructions that tell the hardware what to do. For example, Google Chrome, video games, and the operating system (like Windows or Android) are all software.

2. The 4 Basic Steps of Computing

No matter how advanced a computer is, it always follows these four simple steps:
  1. Input: You give the computer a command. (Example: You type a letter on your keyboard or tap a touchscreen).
  2. Processing: The computer's brain (called the CPU) thinks about your command and calculates what to do next.
  3. Storage: The computer saves data. It uses RAM for things it needs to remember right now (short-term memory) and a Hard Drive/SSD for things it needs to save forever (long-term memory, like photos or files).
  4. Output: The computer shows you the result. (Example: The letter you typed appears on the screen, or music plays out of the speakers).

3. What Counts as a "Computer"?

When people say "computer," they usually think of a laptop or a desktop PC. But computers are actually everywhere around us today:
  • Smartphones: These are just incredibly powerful, pocket-sized computers.
  • Servers: Massive computers that stay turned on 24/7 to host websites, stream videos (like YouTube or Netflix), and run the internet.
  • Embedded Computers: Tiny, specialized chips inside everyday objects like modern cars, smart TVs, washing machines, and traffic lights.


4. The Secret Language of Computers

Humans speak many languages, but computers only understand one thing: electricity.

Inside a computer's brain are billions of tiny switches.

  • If a switch is ON, it represents the number 1.
  • If a switch is OFF, it represents the number 0..

This is called the Binary System. Every single picture, video, word, and game you see on a computer is just billions of 1s and 0s being flipped on and off incredibly fast!





If a computer is a factory, the components are the individual workers and machines that keep the factory running. Computer components are generally divided into two main categories: Internal Components (the parts hidden inside the computer case) and External Components (the peripherals you plug into it).

1. The Core Internal Components

These are the parts that do the heavy lifting, processing, and storing of your data.

  • The Brain: CPU (Central Processing Unit)
  • The Backbone: Motherboard
  • Short-Term Memory: RAM (Random Access Memory)
  • The Artist: GPU (Graphics Processing Unit / Video Card)
